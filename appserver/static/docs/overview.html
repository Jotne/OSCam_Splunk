About the OSCam app for Splunk

This OSCam app provides a hands-on way to see what is going on with your OSCam Server.

Now that you've installed the app, you may like to know what the different dashboards gives you.

Definition

  • Time frame
    The time windows you are looking at. Often set to last 24 hour

  • Time span
    Defining how often a graph shall be updated. Default 10 min.

  • Host
    This is one OSCam server. Splunk can monitor several OSCam severs (hosts) at the same time.

  • Client
    A client connects to you and gets ecm from your host

  • Reader/Proxy(Server)
    Proxy is a local reader or an emualator. Reader is your host connecting to another host to get ecm.
    Both Proxy and Reader are in the Server section of the config files.

Dashboards

  • 1. OSCam Channels Usage Timeline
    What channels are your client looking at? Drill down and you will see.
    If you select one client, you can see what ecm request he makes.

  • 2. OSCam Channels requested
    You see here what channels are decoded by a specific reader/proxy (server) or by Client.
    Have you added a new reader(server) and like to see what channels he give ecm for,
    just select him from the drop down list. If list is blank, he gives nothing.
    You can do the same for a client. Select one and you all request hi has made.

  • 3. OSCam Channels decoded
    Show what channels are decoded or not decoded, sorted by number of hits and by clients.
    You can drill down to host and/or server.

  • 4. OSCam Channels not in oscam.srvid
    Show what channels are not listed in oscam.srvid(2). Update the file exits this list.
    To make most of this dashboards to look nice, you need an oscam.srvid file.
    If a channel is not listed in the file, you see here caid:sid@pvid so you can search or add the id

  • 5. OSCam Clents ecm requests
    Ecm request sorted by hits of Client and by Reader/Proxy(Server) or Channel.

  • 6. OScam Reader Connections activities
    Whats going on with your OSCam server? Some panels are hidden if they show no data.

  • 7. OScam Clients Connections activities
    Whats going on with your OSCam server? Some panels are hidden if they show no data.

  • 8. OSCam Live view
    Here you have two view that shows what information OSCam sends out. Its separated in Ecm and Other info view.
    Ecm gives you all Ecm request OSCam host handles
    Other info shows the rest. Like loadbalance statistics saved. Readers/Clients connecting. Errors etc.

  • 9. OSCam Clients map location
    Not all ISP shows city (shows unknown) but all shows country correct. Zoom in to see better location.

  • 10. OSCam Restarts
    When OSCam restarts, it will create a log entry showing that it starts.
    Second panel shows someone do a soft restart of OSCam from GUI

  • 11. OSCam Overview
    Show a quick view of all the information as numbers
